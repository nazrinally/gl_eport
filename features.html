PLAY GAME
TRAILER
WEEK 1-2

The first two weeks focused on getting organized. Kelvin and I met with our GL teacher to discuss the game concept and created a project timeline. We assigned roles based on our strengths and spent time browsing Unity Asset Store and itch.io for free assets that would work for our roguelike wave defense game.

WEEK 3-4

We started following our development schedule and began implementing the core gameplay. During this time, we encountered our first major bug with the skeleton enemy's attack animation not playing correctly. Kelvin and I spent some time debugging together, going through the code line by line until we found the issue. After fixing it, we noticed similar bugs popping up with other enemies, but we were able to solve them much faster thanks to the experience we gained from the first one.

WEEK 5-6

We held our first open play test and collected feedback through a Google Form. The responses were a bit inadequate since we didn't have much content in the game yet. During these weeks, we also worked on our first unique enemy, the flying eye. It caused us some problems initially, but we applied what we learned from fixing the skeleton enemy's attack animation bug and managed to solve the issues relatively quickly.

WEEK 7-8

These weeks were busy with major additions to the game. We created our first boss enemy and spent time balancing all the enemies to make the gameplay feel fair. We also revamped the UI icons for the shop and upgrades screen. Adding music was a completely new experience for me, but it made the game feel more complete. We also built out the start screen and additional screens like the enemy catalogue and settings menu.

WEEK 9-10

We began working on this e-portfolio to document our development journey. During our consultation with the GL teacher, some unexpected bugs were discovered that we had to fix. We also focused on the final polishing of the game, making sure everything looked and felt as good as possible before wrapping up the project.

WEEK 11-14

The final stretch focused on completing this e-portfolio and getting everything ready for presentation. We prepared for the GL interview, reviewing our development process and what we learned. We also conducted our final open play test to gather last minute feedback and ensure the game was ready for submission.
